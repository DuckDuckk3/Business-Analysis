# II. Academic Foundation: The Pillars of BA
# Overview
The Academic Foundation is designed to build a rigorous mindset. Before a Business Analyst can recommend a strategy, they must understand the underlying logic of markets (Economics), the language of data (Statistics), and the mechanics of systems (Informatics).

## Learning Roadmap
To build a cohesive understanding, subjects should be approached in the following logical order:

1.  **The Logical Layer:** *General Informatics* & *Calculus III*. 
    *   Builds the mental discipline for structured thinking and understanding change/optimization.
2.  **The Contextual Layer:** *Microeconomics* & *Macroeconomics*. 
    *   Establishes how individual agents and entire nations make choices under scarcity.
3.  **The Analytical Layer:** *Probability & Statistics* & *Mathematical Economics*.
    *   Provides the tools to quantify uncertainty and model economic theories into solvable equations.
4.  **The Synthesis Layer:** *Applied Mathematics in Business and Trade*.
    *   Applying all the above to solve real-world commercial problems.

## Key Considerations for the Research Project
*   **Concepts Over Formulas:** Do not focus solely on solving math problems. Focus on what the *result* means for a business (e.g., what does a "Standard Deviation" tell you about supply chain risk?).
*   **Interdisciplinary Links:** Always look for connections. For example, how does *Microeconomics* (Marginal Cost) use *Calculus III* (Derivatives)?
*   **Data Integrity:** As you study Informatics, focus on how data is structured, as this is the primary "raw material" for any BA task.
